## Question
Sometimes we experience “unconscious” errors, and ______ to say words we do not mean to express. 

## Choices
A. manage
B. try
C. prefer
D. likely

## Explanation
The sentence describes unintentionally saying something. "Manage to say words we do not mean" fits best, implying that despite our conscious intent, the unconscious slips out.

## Question
Identify the underlined part that contains an error:
In earlier times(A), criminals were hung(B) in the(C) town plaza(D).

## Choices
A. In earlier times
B. were hung
C. in the
D. town plaza

## Explanation
When referring to the execution of a person, the past tense of "hang" is "hanged," not "hung."

## Question
Identify the underlined part that contains an error:
The surgeon requested for(A) a scalpel, a piece of(B) sterile glove(C), and a mask(D).

## Choices
A. requested for
B. a piece of
C. sterile glove
D. a mask

## Explanation
"Request" is a transitive verb and does not require the preposition "for." It should simply be "requested a scalpel."

## Question
Select the meaning that best fits the context of the underlined phrase:
It goes **against the grain** to have to pay him for such poor work.

## Choices
A. It is against good manners
B. It is against the law
C. It is a waste of money
D. It is irritating

## Explanation
The idiom "against the grain" means contrary to one's natural inclination or feeling. In this sentence, having to pay for poor work is galling or irritating — it goes against what feels right.

## Question
Determine the best order of the following words/phrases to form a logical sentence:
1. apartment
2. blue
3. the student’s
4. is
5. painted

## Choices
A. 5-2-4-3-1
B. 3-1-4-5-2
C. 3-2-1-4-5
D. 4-3-2-1-5

## Explanation
The correct logical order is Subject-Verb-Adjective: "The student's (3) apartment (1) is (4) painted (5) blue (2)."

## Question
Choose the best from among the statements given below:

## Choices
A. He wanted to know if did you win the first prize.
B. He wanted to know if you win the first prize.
C. He wanted to know whether you won the first prize.
D. He wanted to know did you win the first prize.

## Explanation
In an indirect question the subject and verb keep statement order (no inversion), and the tense must match the past tense "wanted." A and D wrongly invert with "did," and B uses the present "win" instead of the past "won." Only C is correct: "whether you won."

## Question
Piliin ang letra ng bahaging may mali:
Ang mahalagang kontribusyon(A) sa pag-unlad ng ating kabuhayan at kultura(B) ang iba’t ibang sektor(C) ng lipunan(D).

## Choices
A. Ang mahalagang kontribusyon
B. sa pag-unlad ng ating kabuhayan at kultura
C. ang iba’t ibang sektor
D. ng lipunan

## Explanation
The sentence structure is inverted and lacks a clear predicate marker. It should be "May mahalagang kontribusyon..." to indicate possession/action by the different sectors.
